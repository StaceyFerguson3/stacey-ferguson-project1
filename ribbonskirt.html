Ribbon Skirt Tutorial

Let's look at what we need to make a ribbon skirt. Below is a checklist for you.

Materials Required














One: Preshrink your fabric

Pre-shrinking is only required for cotton fabric. Satin does not need to be pre-shrunk.

  • Machine was in warm water
  • Dry in the dryer
  • Iron to remove all the wrinkles

Two: Measure

  • Measure your waist and record
  • Measure the length from your waistline down calf level and record

Three: Transfer measurements to fabric

  • Fold fabric in half lengthwise
  • Divide your waist measurement by four (eg. a 36 inch waist divided by 4 is 9 inches)
  • Marking the waist with a ruler, draw a straight line from the fold to 9 inches and add 4 inches bringing the total to 13 inches
  • Mark the length from the waist to hem adding 2 inches for the waistband and 2 inches for the hem (4 in total)
  • Draw a line across the fabric that measures the width of the first line drawn for the waist and add 6 inches so the bottom is wider than the waist (eg. if the waist is 13 inches add 6 inches for a total of 9 inches)

Four: Cutting the fabric

  • Cut out the first panel
  • Use the first panel to create a second, identical panel
  • You now have two panel pieces, one for the front and one for the back

Five: Ribbon time!

  • Lay your fabric on a flat surface
  • Measure and mark 4 inches, or your desired height, above the hem for the ribbon placement
  • Draw a line across the width of the skirt
  • Determine ribbon length needed from edge to edge with extra and cut
  • Use your adhesive spray to hold ribbon in place
  • Sew the bottom of the ribbon with your sewing machine, following the line.
  • Sew the top of the ribbon down after the bottom is sewn down
  • Proceed to stitch the next ribbon on by either stitching directly above the first ribbon or measure a distance between ribbons
  • Follow these steps to apply each ribbon to front and back panels
  • Trim and lightly singe the edge of the ribbons to prevent fraying and unraveling

Six: Sew the skirt together

  • Once ribbons are attached, place front and back panels together. You will have the ribbons on the inside facing together
  • Line up your ribbons and use straight pins to hold both panels together
  • Using a sewing machine, stitch the side seams together. Sew half and inch to prevent seam splitting.
  • Using the iron, press seams flat

Seven: Let's get the waistband assembled

  • Fold the top of the waist material down 2 inches and iron
  • Make a 5/8 inch fold and iron to make a hem for the waistband
  • Stitch along the small fold along the width of the waist leaving a 2 inch space opening for the elastic to be inserted
  • Use your bodkin or large safety pin to thread the elastic through the opening
  • Elastic should be the same length as the original waist measurement but I always make it an inch smaller so the skirt fits snugly but not tight
  • Stitch the ends of the elastic together and stitch opening closed

Eight: Hem the skirt

  • Fold the bottom of the skirt up 2 inches and iron
  • Make a 5/8 inch fold and iron to make a nice hem for the skirt
  • Stitch along the small fold along the width of the hem

You're all done!
