A Bit About Me

My name is Stacey Ferguson. I am a daughter, wife, mother, grandmother, sister, auntie and friend. I am Coast Salish from qiqeyt first nation as well as Chinese and Scottish.

Learning to sew was one of the first things I was taught as my grandmother, Marie, great-Aunt Dorothy and my mom, Rhonda were all skilled seamstresses who made everything for everyone in the family. Here they are in stylish matching outfits they made in the early 1940's. Look at the gloves!

My mom started teaching me to sew when I was around five years old. My wish for the future when I graduated high school was to be a fashion designer and in a crazy way, I am.

My kids' dad was from Treaty 6 territory and a pow wow dancer. In order to supplement our finances on the pow wow trail with his winnings, we started Traditional Tots and made regalia with emphasis on regalia for children. We didn't make any money but we made some really wicked trades.

As our kids got older, my sewing skills were utilized on costumes for every occasion. Halloween, Valentine's Day, Christmas, Easter, school plays and lots of just because. Now that they're adults, my skills are utilized for cosplay (Hatsune Miku or Obi-Wan Kenobi, anyone?).
